Abstract

The quality of underwater images is often degraded by phenomena like light absorption and scattering, reducing visibility and distorting colors. These challenges hinder applications in marine exploration and environmental monitoring. We propose the Gated Fusion Network (GFN), integrating a Swin Transformer and Reprogramming Adaptive Transformation Units (RATUs). GFN dynamically adapts to diverse underwater conditions, leveraging advanced feature fusion and context-aware transformations. Evaluations on benchmark datasets, such as EUVP and LSUI400, highlight its superior performance, including a 3.1 dB PSNR improvement over WaterNet. These findings demonstrate GFN's capability to advance underwater imaging for practical scenarios.
